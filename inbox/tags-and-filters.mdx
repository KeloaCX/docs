Tags are free-form labels you add to conversations. They power filters, analytics, and assignment rules.

## Add a tag

- In a conversation: press <kbd>t</kbd> or click the **+** next to tags in the header, and type a name.
- In bulk: select conversations, click **Tag** in the bulk bar.

Tags are free-form — typing a new one creates it. The workspace auto-completes from tags already in use.

## Remove a tag

Click the × next to a tag in the conversation header. Or bulk-remove the same way you bulk-add.

## Built-in filters

At the top of the conversation list:

- **All** — every open conversation
- **Mine** — assigned to you
- **AI** — handled by AI (no human assignee)
- **SLA** — approaching response-time breach
- **Resolved** — already closed

## Filter by tag

Click any tag pill in the header to pin it as a filter — you'll see only conversations carrying that tag. Useful for triaging launch-day surges, campaign replies, or a single product line.

## Tagging patterns that work

- **Intent tags** — `returns`, `shipping`, `sales`, `bug`. Feed analytics.
- **Priority tags** — `vip`, `urgent`. Drive assignment rules.
- **Campaign tags** — `blackfriday-2026`. Temporary; archive when the campaign ends.

## Auto-tagging with flows

A flow with a **Condition** node + an **Action** node can tag conversations automatically — e.g. *if the message contains "refund", add tag `returns`*. See [Flows](/flows/overview).

## Analytics

Tags are a primary grouping in **Analytics** — you can see CSAT, resolution time, and AI deflection broken down per tag. So think of them as your own taxonomy, not just labels.
